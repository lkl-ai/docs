02 — Why this matters

How this ties to LOKAL's North Stars

This partnership isn't a revenue moonshot. It's the primary vehicle for the Service-as-a-Software transition.

MRR

₱400k/month protected (defensive moat through deeper integration). Upsell to ~₱500–550k with AI products. More importantly: co-marketing engine (Academy, newsletter, roundtables) generates pipeline for new hotel clients at ₱25–50k MRR each.

Gross margin

This is the big one. Current ₱400k is services — human time, low margin. Every AI product shifts the delivery from labor to software. Same revenue, lower cost to deliver. GM copilot, voice AI, Ely — all software-economics. The path from ~40% margin to 70%+ runs through HII.

AI-delivered %

Every AI product directly increases this. Copilot replaces human reporting. Voice AI replaces human phone answering. Ely replaces human chat. Cross-property intel replaces manual CRM. HII's 16 properties give us the proof point: "X% of guest communication is now AI-delivered."

Direct booking %

Booking engine at Club Nalu is the direct play. Ely chat-to-booking conversion is the other. Rev-share model (if we pursue it) aligns our incentive directly with this metric. HII's 16 properties are a huge chunk of our denominator — moving their direct ratio moves our North Star.

The real story: HII is the lab where we prove Service-as-a-Software works. We're using their portfolio to build and validate the products that turn LOKAL from a services company into a software-delivered services company. The AI products we build here get packaged and sold to every hotel after. HII pays for services today while we build the software that delivers the same value tomorrow at 80%+ margin.

04 — AI products

Built inside HII's portfolio, sold to the market

16 live hotels is a development lab. Everything that works gets packaged.

01

GM operations copilot

Chat-based hotel performance intelligence

GM opens a chat, asks "how did we do this week?" — instant analysis of occupancy, revenue, guest sentiment, social mentions, Ely trends, competitor pricing. Replaces hours of manual weekly reporting across 16 properties.

Extension of the marketing portal. Could be a premium tier. Test at Club Nalu, roll to portfolio, then sell as standalone to non-HII hotels.

Dependency: IDB PMS API access. If the API is weak, this stalls. Scope integration first. Confidence: 75% if data pipeline works.

02

Voice AI for phone inquiries

Ely answers the phone — 24/7

Voice version of Ely that handles phone calls in natural conversation. FAQs, booking inquiries, directions, hours. Escalates to human for complex requests. No Philippine hotel management company offers this.

Test at one English-heavy property first (Aurora Subic or Harvest Hotel). Quality bar is high — robotic voice = brand damage.

Risk: Taglish/Filipino language mixing could be rough with current voice AI. Start English-only. Confidence: 50% for broad deployment, higher for English-tourist-heavy properties.

03

Cross-property guest intelligence

Shared AI memory across the portfolio

Guest preferences, communication history, and booking patterns travel across all 16 HII properties. Boracay guest who likes quiet rooms → flagged at The Connor six months later.

Only works with a multi-property portfolio and a shared AI layer. Genuine competitive moat. But value depends on cross-property repeat booking rate — if only 2% of guests book multiple HII properties, the infrastructure serves a thin use case. Needs to be paired with active cross-selling (post-stay emails promoting other HII destinations).

Dependency: PMS guest data access across properties. Privacy/consent framework. Confidence: 35% standalone, 65% if paired with cross-property marketing program.

07 — The honest assessment

The imbalance — and what to do about it

Laying it out straight for the partners.

LOKAL is doing ~80% of the work. All technology — build, host, maintain. All content production. All data analysis and AI development. Marketing execution. Email platforms. Workshop curriculum. Follow-up sequences. This is 3-4 team members' worth of capacity plus significant dev time on AI products.
HII is providing access to assets they already have. Properties (empty function rooms), people (in slack hours), data (that sits in systems anyway), a contact list (that already exists), and brand credibility (that costs them nothing to lend). Their marginal cost is low. Our marginal cost is high.

HII's contribution is necessary — we can't do any of this without their data, venues, and credibility. But necessary is not proportional. They're lending keys to rooms that are already empty. We're building products from scratch.

What we get despite the imbalance

Defensive moat

Protect the ₱4.8M/year. AI products baked into their operations make it progressively harder for HII to leave or replace us. A chatbot is a widget. A GM copilot their managers rely on daily is infrastructure.

Service-as-a-Software proof

The 16-property portfolio is where we prove the model works. Real data, real metrics, real case study. This proof is what lets us sell the next 50 hotels at software margins.

Modest upsell

₱400k → ~₱500-550k/month. ₱80-120k in new AI product subscriptions. Not transformative, but real incremental revenue at high margin.

Lead generation pipeline

Academy, newsletter, LinkedIn, Readiness Playbook — all generate warm leads for new hotel clients. 3-4 new hotels over 6 months = ₱100-200k/month new MRR.

What we should ask for to rebalance

1. Financial co-investment

HII co-funds the Digital Readiness Study, Academy launch costs, and any summit/event production. These activities generate leads for HII's management business too — they should pay for part of the lead gen. Not 50/50 necessarily, but skin in the game.

2. Dedicated HII counterpart

Not "Dinna when she has time." A named person whose job includes this partnership — attends planning calls, owns guest lists, coordinates venues, is accountable for HII's deliverables. Without this, everything depends on busy executives fitting it in around their real jobs, and HII's side consistently slips.

3. Exclusivity on marketing + tech

If we're building AI products inside their portfolio and the RDS tech stack they sell to new clients, we should be the exclusive marketing/technology partner. No shopping around. No "let's also talk to this other agency." Lock it in for the duration of the partnership.

4. Revenue commitment on RDS

When HII signs RDS clients using the tech stack we built, LOKAL isn't a vendor they can negotiate down. Lock in the revenue split upfront — before the first RDS client signs. We built the products. We should have a guaranteed position in the commercial model.

5. Equity or rev-share on AI products

If the GM copilot, voice AI, or cross-property intelligence become products HII uses to win new management contracts, we should participate in that upside. Not just a flat fee for the software — a share of the value it creates. This is the JV conversation.

6. Full use of their physical assets

Free venues for all co-marketing events (roundtables, Academy, summit). Discounted/comp accommodation for attendees and LOKAL team travel. F&B for all events at cost. Properties as content production locations (video, photo). Work Folk co-working access for LOKAL Manila activities. This is low-cost for HII (empty rooms, existing kitchens) but ₱200-300k/month in value if we had to pay market rates.

7. Permission to tell the story — publicly, with real data

Case studies, video series, Readiness Playbook, Academy materials — all depend on being able to say "we do this for HII's 16 properties and here are the results." Real numbers. Real property names. Real before-and-after. Explicit agreement upfront, not asked for permission case by case.

09 — Decisions

What we need to align on before going back to HII

Questions for RK and CJ.

01

How hard do we push on exclusivity?

If we're building their RDS tech stack and AI products, we should be exclusive. But asking for exclusivity changes the tone from "partnership" to "lock-in." How aggressive do we want to be?

02

JV or contract?

A JV formalizes shared IP and upside on the AI products. A contract keeps it flexible. JV makes sense if we believe this is a 3-5 year play. Contract if we want to stay nimble.

03

Rev-share or flat fees for AI products?

Rev-share on direct bookings aligns incentives and removes budget objections. But it's variable and harder to forecast. Flat subscription is predictable MRR. Could offer both — let properties choose.

04

How much do we ask HII to co-fund?

The study, the Academy launch, summit costs. Do we ask for 50/50? 30/70? Or do we eat the cost and treat it as marketing investment for the leads it generates?

05

What goes in Dinna's proposal?

She asked for one. What do we commit to, what do we present as options, what do we hold back? Do we lead with the co-marketing or the AI products? How much of the "ask" do we put in writing vs. discuss verbally?

06

Team capacity

This is 3-4 team members' worth of capacity plus dev time. Do we have it? What deprioritizes? Or does HII's ₱400k/month justify dedicated resourcing?
